# Lesson 02: Cloud Computing Fundamentals

## Learning Objectives

- Understand cloud computing.
- Understand IaaS, PaaS and SaaS.
- Understand elasticity.
- Understand scalability.
- Understand managed services.

---

## What Is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing provides computing resources on demand.

Examples:

- servers
- storage
- databases
- networking

Delivered over the internet.

---

## Traditional Infrastructure

Organization buys:

- servers
- networking
- storage

Manages everything.

---

## Cloud Infrastructure

Provider manages infrastructure.

Customers consume resources.

Examples:

- AWS
- Azure
- GCP

---

## IaaS

Infrastructure As A Service.

Examples:

- EC2
- DigitalOcean Droplets

Customer manages operating systems and applications.

---

## PaaS

Platform As A Service.

Examples:

- Render
- Heroku

Provider manages more infrastructure.

Developers focus on applications.

---

## SaaS

Software As A Service.

Examples:

- Gmail
- Notion
- Slack

Users consume finished software.

---

## Elasticity

Resources increase or decrease automatically.

Example:

Traffic spike.

↓

Additional instances launched.

---

## Scalability

Ability to handle growth.

Horizontal:

More instances.

Vertical:

Bigger instance.

---

## Managed Services

Examples:

- managed databases
- managed Redis
- managed Kubernetes

Reduce operational burden.

---

## Benefits

- flexibility
- global reach
- reduced upfront cost

---

## Risks

- vendor lock-in
- cost management
- operational complexity

---

## Portfolio Examples

SyncGrid:

- Render
- Neon
- Upstash

VaultBox:

- Render
- PostgreSQL
- Redis

These are cloud-native architectures.

---

## Summary

Cloud computing provides flexible, scalable infrastructure and forms the foundation of modern platform engineering.
