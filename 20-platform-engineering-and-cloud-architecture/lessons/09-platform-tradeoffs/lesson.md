# Lesson 09: Platform Trade-Offs

## Learning Objectives

- Evaluate platform decisions.
- Understand operational trade-offs.
- Understand build vs buy decisions.
- Understand platform complexity.
- Understand organizational trade-offs.

---

## Introduction

Platform engineering is about making trade-offs.

There are rarely perfect solutions.

Every decision has costs and benefits.

Good platform engineers understand those trade-offs.

---

## Build vs Buy

Question:

Should we build a platform?

Or buy one?

Examples:

Buy:

- Render
- Vercel
- Heroku

Build:

- Internal platform
- NOMMO
- Custom orchestration

---

## Buying Advantages

- faster delivery
- reduced maintenance
- lower operational burden

---

## Buying Disadvantages

- vendor lock-in
- limited customization
- pricing constraints

---

## Building Advantages

- flexibility
- control
- customization

---

## Building Disadvantages

- engineering effort
- operational overhead
- maintenance costs

---

## Platform Complexity

Every platform introduces complexity.

Examples:

- networking
- security
- monitoring
- deployment automation

Complexity should solve real problems.

---

## Standardization

Benefits:

- consistency
- easier onboarding
- predictable operations

---

## Flexibility

Benefits:

- team autonomy
- innovation

Too much flexibility can create chaos.

---

## Centralization

Benefits:

- governance
- visibility

Costs:

- bottlenecks
- slower change

---

## Decentralization

Benefits:

- autonomy
- speed

Costs:

- inconsistency

---

## Platform Success Metrics

Examples:

- deployment frequency
- lead time
- reliability
- developer satisfaction

---

## NOMMO Analysis

Advantages:

- control
- orchestration experience
- platform engineering learning

Costs:

- maintenance
- operational complexity

---

## Common Mistakes

- building platforms too early
- ignoring developer experience
- excessive complexity
- lack of standardization

---

## Summary

Platform engineering is a balance between control, simplicity, flexibility and operational cost.
